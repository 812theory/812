# I Am Not a God

I am not a god. Yes, I've survived multiple accidents that would have killed normal humans, but every single one of those was completely random and I can claim no credit. Surviving being blown out of an airplane by falling into a just-launched weather balloon, for example. Random.

I am not a god. Yes, I once punched a building and it came tumbling down, but that was apparently due to a ruptured fuel line under the building or somesuch. Authorities were never quite clear on what the cause was, but they were quite sure that wasn't some random dude in the throes of grief over a recent breakup. Very random.

I am not a god. Yes, I once piloted a mining hauler through an asteroid storm without any piloting experience, instruments, or visibility, but it later came to light that the on-board AI had become sentient and simply didn't want to die due to my stupidity. Obscenely random.

No, I am not a god. But, for some reason, I've been alive for thousands of years and over those years many random things have happened to me. I've lived dozens of lives. I've loved and been loved, walked the surface of every country that's ever existed, and seen mankind at its greatest and its most barren.

I thought I'd seen it all, and that the things people have said about me over the years were just the culmination of so much randomness spread over so many years. That is until he showed up on my doorstep.

To be clear it is a rather unremarkable doorstep. While I've accumulated wealth over the lifetimes, showing it off gives me no joy, so I live in a simple bungalow outside of Toronto, Canada. Not the best weather, but of all of the delicacies I've sampled over the centuries, poutine really is right up there.

Unremarkable doorstep attached to an unremarkable house belonging to an unremarkable dude. That's where the "uns" ended. While his physical attributes weren't anything special — maybe 6 feet tall, average build, gray hair cropped short and professional — his clothes were off. As if someone on an LSD trip had tried describing normal "human-style clothes" to him, but he'd been on a separate LSD trip while listening.

Oversized sandals with bright green socks. Finely tailored suit pants with his tighty whities worn over top. A women's sized American Eagle crop top, pink. Gloves clearly worn inside out. Fedora, with various little flowers pinned to the top.

As soon as I opened the door, he rushed inside, slammed it closed, looked me up and down and then booped my nose as I passed rapidly into darkness.

---

Waking up was harder than normal. My eyes felt heavy, my head felt heavy. Everything felt heavy.

"Oh thank goodness, you're up, here let me help you," said a strangely distant voice, as I felt myself hoisted to a sitting position.

My eyes adjusted and opened, taking in the enclosed space around me. Slightly spherical in nature, it had a little bit of everything attached to the walls. Random magazine covers, computer keyboards, even a faded Ronald McDonald sitting in the corner. It was a mess, eclectic and yet also strangely familiar. Filled with things any normal human would instantly recognize.

Struggling to take it all in, I looked back to the intruder. He was clearly nervous and jittery. Rubbing his hands together, pacing, and muttering to himself.

"Where are we, who are you, and what the flying fuck is going on?!" I probably could have not shouted the last part. "I'm scared, confused, and could really really use some information!"

"You think you're scared? I just kidnapped a GOD!"
