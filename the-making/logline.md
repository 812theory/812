# 8:12 THEORY — LOGLINE & PITCH

*This is probably the oldest artifact in the repo. Written before the pitch bible, before v1 went to coverage, before the thirteen rules existed. The first attempt to say what the show was in one sentence.*

*What's interesting for writers: the logline held. The comp triangle held. The pitch paragraph held. Everything around it — the rules, the camera arc, the chime grammar, the visual bible, eight episodes — was built to make this sentence true.*

*This is where it started.*

---

## LOGLINE

A grief-stricken structural engineer in Bangkok investigates buildings that shouldn't still be standing — and discovers the city itself is trying to tell him something about the daughter he lost.

---

## PITCH (One Paragraph)

Met Somchai is a structural integrity investigator in Bangkok — a genius who can look at a cracked wall and tell you where the foundation shifted, what year the soil moved, and how long before the building falls. He solves cases that no one else can solve. But the cases keep getting stranger. Buildings that should have collapsed years ago. Spaces that don't match their blueprints. Rooms that feel wrong in ways his instruments can't measure. His new partner, Chloe — an Australian systems consultant with her own reasons for being in Bangkok — sees patterns in his closed cases that he refuses to see. His office manager Ice has been filing two sets of records for years and won't say why. His dog Unit tilts his head at empty corners and stares at spaces where nothing is. And every morning at 8:12, for exactly one second, the entire city of Bangkok goes silent. Met is the only person who notices. 8:12 THEORY is a show about what happens when a man built to solve problems encounters one that solving won't fix — because the answer isn't in the math. It's in the thing he's been subtracting from every equation since his daughter disappeared.

---

## COMP TRIANGLE

- **Severance** — workplace as existential horror, mundane details that mean everything, slow-burn mystery where the audience knows more than the characters think they do
- **Undone** — grief as reality distortion, a protagonist who can't tell if they're broken or the world is, the question of whether the impossible is real or a symptom of damage
- **Guardians of the Galaxy** — found family formed by broken people, humour that makes the devastation hit harder, a soundtrack that carries emotional weight the characters can't speak

---

## ELEVATOR VERSIONS

**10 seconds:** Structural engineer in Bangkok. Buildings that can't stand, do. His dead daughter might be why.

**30 seconds:** Met is a genius structural investigator in Bangkok who solves impossible building cases. But his new partner sees something in his closed files — the cases aren't wrong, they're incomplete. The buildings shouldn't stand. Something is holding them up. And it might be connected to the daughter he lost seven years ago. His dog already knows.

**For the room:** This is a show about a man who is very, very good at solving problems — so good that he's been solving the wrong problem for seven years and no one noticed. Not even him. Especially not him. The city of Bangkok is trying to tell him something. His dog hears it. His new partner sees it. The buildings he investigates are screaming it. And every morning at 8:12, for one second, the whole city goes quiet — like it's waiting for him to finally listen.
