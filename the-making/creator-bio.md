# JAY WRIGHT — CREATOR BIO

*Written March 2026, assembled alongside the pitch bible. Multiple versions for different contexts — the room, the query letter, the one-liner. ## Pitch Bible Version

Jay Wright. Creator and consultant. Created HUMANITY — original IP sold to Netflix twice, most recently for adult animation. Plotline and continuity consultant on Strange New Worlds for 3.5 seasons. Visual consultant on Bright and other Netflix projects — frame-level easter egg architecture, the kind designed for the rewatch community and the people going frame-by-frame on YouTube. Visiting professor at seven universities including Stanford, Cornell, and LSE. 15 years building audience systems — a career in audience architecture that informs how this show is structurally designed to generate rewatch, community, and cultural conversation. Has lived and worked in Bangkok. Knows the city, the culture, the production landscape, and the specific sound a 7-11 makes at 2 AM when you can't sleep and the world feels slightly wrong.

8:12 THEORY is his most personal project. The trauma in Met's wound is real. The recovery is real. The found family is real. This is a show about what happens when you stop subtracting the things that don't fit.

## Elevator Add-On (spoken, not written)

"I built the easter egg architecture for Bright at Netflix — around thirty frame-level details designed for rewatch and the YouTube frame-by-frame community. What I learned is that hiding things inside a story is fun, but hiding the story's meaning inside things the audience already dismissed is something else entirely. That's what 8:12 is built to do."

## Room Context (spoken, when asked "why you?")

"I've lived in Bangkok. I ran a digital agency across Southeast Asia. I taught at Bangkok University. I helped rescue 1500 dogs, 3000 cats, and 150 elephants through an NGO I co-founded there. This isn't a western writer's idea of Bangkok from a hotel lobby. I know what the city sounds like at 8:12 in the morning because I've stood on that corner."
