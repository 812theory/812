## SESSION 1 — GRUMPY EXEC

**Purpose:** Gut check. Jay sensed development sessions were running warm. Needed cold eyes on everything at once.

### WHAT THE EXEC GOT RIGHT (Survived all four rounds)

**E1 case solves too fast.**
One pass. Met walks in, touches wall, delivers monologue, done. Needs three investigative passes. This is the procedural door. The door needs hinges. Most important single fix.

**Ice is underwritten relative to importance.**
Every Ice scene in E1-E3 observed through glass/walls. Break the rule once. Early. Give her thirty seconds that belong to her, not the camera.

**E5 top-heavy.**
Dissolution gets four layers. Recomposition gets a sketch. Ratio problem. Two more pages in Movement 5.

**E2 weakest episode.**
Fragments only in current package. Needs to be as strong as E1, E3, E4.

**Met passivity risk.**
Contained protagonist vs. passive protagonist is a frame difference, not a risk difference. Need more visible containment-under-pressure moments in E2-E3. Active stillness, not inertia.

**Annotations fused into prose.**
The prosey scripts explain the experience they're creating. "The audience who catches it will..." sentences need to move out.

---

### WHAT THE EXEC GOT WRONG (Corrected through discussion)

**Procedural engine as failure.**
Initially diagnosed the show as a broken procedural. After seeing audience analysis (P1 as door, not destination), corrected: the procedural is a sales tool, not the show. But the door still needs to work as a door — hence E1 fix.

**Met as passive protagonist.**
Initially diagnosed as passive. Corrected to contained. But the correction doesn't eliminate the screen risk. Solution: more physical containment beats, not more plot action.

**Steve needs to be weirder.**
Wanted Steve stranger in first scene. After reading character bible, corrected: Steve's normalcy IS the point. The grin is professional. If Steve seems weird, Met doesn't sit down.

**Supernatural question must be explicit in pitch.**
Initially wanted mechanism declared. After understanding the mythology is deliberately hidden, corrected: have a prepared one-sentence answer for the room, but don't put it in the document.

---

### THREE DISCOVERIES (Emerged mid-read, unprompted)

**The three-cast model.**
- Cast 1 (Actors): 25 words. Dialogue sides. Their confusion IS Met's confusion.
- Cast 2 (Camera + Sound): 12,000 words. The prosey script is their dialogue.
- Cast 3 (Crew): 12 rules. The showrunner speech is their director's brief.
- Cast 4 (Audience): Their script is the show itself. They don't know they're performing until the rewatch.

The prosey scripts aren't overwritten. They're written to the density their cast requires. The issue isn't length — it's routing. Four streams in one pipe.

**The process-as-pitch.**
The long pitch document does to the reader what the show does to the audience. The exec who reads it all and leans forward is the right exec. The exec who wants the five-page version was never going to protect the face rule. But: still need a one-page entry point to get the long pitch read. The short version is the door. The long version is the house.

**The prose escalation arc.**
The prosey scripts escalate in immersion from E1 to E4. E1 prose is professional distance. E4 prose is inside a dog's body on Khao San Road. The transition is invisible until you see it. The exec was processed through the funnel without signing a permission slip.
