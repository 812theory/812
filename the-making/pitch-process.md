## THE PORTFOLIO CONTEXT

Three projects. One journey. Essential context before any room.

**BAD MONKEYS**
https://bad-monkeys-movie.lovable.app/
High-concept sci-fi comedy. Earth is a typo in an alien optimization system. A mechanic from Alberta saves it by treating a broken truck like a person. *Guardians of the Galaxy* meets *Arrival*. Commercially packaged, emotionally real underneath. Franchise potential. This is the writer who knows how the industry works.

**DEAR SATAN**
https://dear-satan-uf670v6.gamma.site/
High-concept holiday comedy. A Corporate Devil saves a five-year-old from a hyper-optimized AI Santa. *The LEGO Movie* meets *Deadpool*. Four-quadrant. The villain is optimization culture — efficiency as the actual monster. This is the writer who can execute a pitch in a room and make it look easy.

**8:12 THEORY (ORIGINAL MOVIE)**
https://the-812-theory-6ef7dwp.gamma.site/
The seed. Met as a risk auditor whose "Auditor's Eye" perceives reality as logical equations — and who is unknowingly holding the world together through denial. The Static as visual metaphor. Ghost Tower climax. Logline: *"When the universe stops rendering, someone has to pay the bill."* This is the movie that became the series when the script was lost and the writer went through the ICU and came back different.

## THE THROUGH-LINE

All three projects are about the same thing wearing different costumes:

- *Broken doesn't mean worthless* (Bad Monkeys)
- *Our flaws — our typos — are what make us human* (Dear Satan)
- *A man who can't let broken things go* (8:12)

The grief in 8:12 isn't new. It was always underneath. The ICU removed the last layer of scaffolding between the writer and the material. The series is what's left when the protection is gone.

This is not a liability in a pitch. This is the answer to "why you, why this, why now."

## THE PITCH SEQUENCE

**Do not lead with the series bible. Lead with the movie.**

### STAGE 1 — THE DOOR (one page)
The movie version. The logline. The hook. The visual.
- *"When the universe stops rendering, someone has to pay the bill."*
- Met. Bangkok. The Static. The Ghost Tower.
- Comp: *Dark* meets *Arrival* with the visual language of *In the Mood for Love.*
- Goal: Get the document on the desk. Get the meeting extended.

### STAGE 2 — THE REALITY (the series bible)
The series version. The grief underneath. The eight episodes. The architecture.
- "Here's what it became when the script was lost and I came back from the ICU."
- The delta between the movie pitch and the series bible IS the pitch for the series.
- The exec who reads the bible all the way through and leans forward is the right exec.
- Goal: The funnel filters. The right person self-selects.

### STAGE 3 — THE ROOM
Three answers that must be ready before any meeting:

**The casting optics answer:**
"Met is foreign by design. His outsider status is what allows him to read Bangkok structurally — to see the bones of the city without the cultural acclimation that makes it invisible to people who live inside it. His foreignness is the instrument, not the conceit. The show is deeply Thai. Met is the audience's way in."

**The supernatural answer:**
"The show lives in the space where grief becomes physics. Whether the Static is literal is the question the audience holds for eight episodes. The answer is yes. But the show earns that answer — it doesn't give it away."

**The 'will you be bored' answer:**
"This is the most personal thing I've ever written. I came back from losing everything and built something that tells the truth about grief without flinching. I'm not going anywhere."

## THE SEQUENCING LOGIC

The grumpy exec cold read validated this: the long pitch IS the audition. The exec who reads E1-E4 and leans forward has been processed through the funnel. They wanted to leave at E3. They couldn't. That experience is the show's argument for itself.

But the funnel only works if someone picks up the document. That's the one-page door.

Bad Monkeys and Dear Satan establish range. Jay Wright can write commercially. He's not a precious indie auteur who only makes things for himself. He knows how the industry works and he's chosen to make something personal anyway. That's a different conversation than "this is my first serious project." That's a writer with receipts choosing to go deeper.

The ICU is context, not content. You don't lead with it. It answers "why now" if the exec asks. It doesn't need to be in the pitch. The work carries it.
