## CAMERA MODES IN E2

### Standard Met-cam (Beats 1, 3, 4, 7, 8)
Default show perspective. Follows Met's eyes. In investigation scenes, becomes DIAGNOSTIC POV — camera as investigative instrument rather than narrative explainer.

**Diagnostic POV spec:** No floating text. No mind palace. No graphical overlays. Met looks at a crack — the camera shows the crack, same angle. His eyes move — the camera follows. A tap on concrete — the SOUND tells you the density. The audience discovers clues simultaneously with Met. The half-second between audience catch and Met's articulation is the show's signature experience.

**Differentiation from comps:** Sherlock, House, Psych, Good Doctor all display the RESULT of genius thinking. 8:12 invites the audience to DO the thinking through Met's eyes.

### Margarita Storm Camera (Beat 9)
First time the camera shifts from OBSERVER to having an OPINION. Editorializes through framing:
- Steve off-center, never the focus. Peripheral presence.
- The GLASS between Met and Steve is the focal point.
- "Not the real capital" — camera holds on the glass. Stillness as reaction.
- Steve alone after Met leaves — camera finally looks at Steve directly for the first time.

### Unit (Beat 2 — BONES ONLY in E2)
No special camera treatment. Standard coverage at Met's distance. The audience watches Unit from the OUTSIDE. Restraint is deliberate.

**FULL UNIT-CAM deploys in E3 or E4.** Specs:
- First non-human perspective. GoPro-on-tail or Unit-eye POV.
- Warmer palette, more saturated. Not stylized — MORE REAL.
- Low camera position (dog-height). City enormous, alive.
- Sound mix fuller than any other scene. Every sound distinct — orchestra, not cacophony.
- Unit looks back at Met twice (hierarchy reveal).
- Sound strips layer by layer as Unit runs. One layer remains: ghost-frequency child's laugh.
- Bangkok SLAMS back at full volume. Unit flinches.
- Return to Met's flattened Bangkok. Audience feels the loss.

### Sathorn Unique — Street Level Only (Beats 4, 6, 7)
Building presented from the ground. No aerial. No spectacle. Scale through human bodies being small against it. Save vertical reveal for E8.

## SOUND DESIGN

### The 8:12 Silence (Beats 1, 11)
One second. Everything stops. Resumes. In Beat 11 (Unit's reaction): faintest possible sub-bass hum. Felt more than heard. Presence in the silence.

### The 7-11 Chime
- Bungalow (Beat 8): Still wrong. Same wrongness as E1. Persistent, not escalating. This is how things ARE near Met.
- Margarita Storm (Beat 9): Correct version. The wrongness is localized to Met's immediate environment, not citywide.

### Sathorn Unique Interior
Concrete spaces echo based on density. Met's tapping IS the data. Real concrete sounds different at different densities. Trust it.

32nd floor wall (Beat 7): ambient sound shifts subtly. Building hums differently. Not dramatic.
