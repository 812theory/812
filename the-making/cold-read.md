## HARD FIXES — Survived all four rounds without softening

### 1. E1 case solves too fast
**Status: Other episode — not E5**
One pass. Met walks in, touches wall, delivers monologue, done. Needs three investigative passes: fast first read that looks like a solve, contradiction that reopens (Chloe's presence complicates), final solve that satisfies but leaves the gap only the camera sees. Most important single fix in the package. The procedural door needs to work as a door for ten minutes before anything else can work.

### 2. Ice needs an unmediated moment before E4
**Status: Other episode — not E5**
Every Ice scene in E1-E3 observed through glass, walls, other cameras. Break the rule once. Early. Give her thirty seconds where the camera is in the room with her on her own terms. Not for the audience — for the actress. The sigh through the wall is the camera's moment, not Ice's. She needs something that belongs to her so the filing cabinet and degaussing have a foundation.

### 3. E5 recomposition is starved
**Status: APPLIES TO E5 v4 — flagged in script with [NOTE FOR WORK SESSION] markers**
Dissolution gets four fully realized layers. Recomposition gets a page and a half. Need two more pages in Movement 5. The porch scene, the morning, the "different" beat — they need room. Not more story. More air. The audience needs to set the fold down before the episode ends.

Specific gaps:
- Porch scene: Steve's cost needs a full beat. What he does with his hands. The quality of a man sitting in what the tickets bought.
- Morning scene: Met's "different" lands but doesn't breathe. Needs a physical action after the word. The show earns "different" and then doesn't let it sit.
- Return train: Currently adequate. Holds its weight.

### 4. E5 Chloe discovery needs a breath before it
**Status: APPLIES TO E5 v4 — flagged in script with [NOTE FOR WORK SESSION] marker**
The fold's emotional debris is still falling when 12B arrives. Need a beat between — even ten seconds of Bangkok ambient, the city existing outside the bathroom. Then Chloe. Then the shape rings instead of getting swallowed.

Note: The hard cut IS the camera's survival reflex and it's right. The breath isn't softening the cut — it's a scene break. City ambient. Then office. The cut stays hard. The breath goes between the cut and the office.

### 5. E2 is the weakest episode
**Status: Other episode — not E5**
Either not fully written yet or thin. Holds the audience after the pilot. The Kitty-Chloe video call is the best naturalistic dialogue in the series — build around it. The case needs to be as present as E1's and E4's.

### 6. Met needs more visible containment-under-pressure in E2 and E3
**Status: Other episodes — not E5**
Active stillness, not passivity. Physical moments where the body betrays the machine. The coat hook is one. The shelf reach is one. Need three more. Small. The audience needs to feel that something is being held and the holding is effort, not nature. Without these, contained reads as passive on screen for five episodes regardless of design philosophy. This is a real risk.

## DOCUMENT ROUTING — Pipe separation
**Status: APPLIES TO E5 v4 — work session structural note**

The prosey scripts are one pipe carrying four streams. They need to be separated:

1. **DP/sound script (the prosey):** Every line that tells camera what it believes, every sound design note, every sensory description that's blocking for non-human cast. This is their dialogue. Stays dense.

2. **Audience-experience scaffolding:** "First watch / On rewatch" blocks, "the audience will feel..." passages, Reddit prediction language. Move to private showrunner bible. These informed the building. They're not the building.

3. **The constitution:** Face rule, sound rule, threshold rule, head-tilt continuity. Standalone document. Framed. Wall. Day one for every department head. The E5 handoff notes are the seed — expand to full series.

4. **Actor sides:** Twenty-five words. Separate document. They're audience two.

Note: v4 still carries all four streams. This separation is the work session task, not a pre-session fix.
