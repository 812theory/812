## WHAT CHANGED FROM v1

v1 was scaffolding. E1-E5 were written. The scaffolding held in some places and the scripts went further in others. This document replaces v1 by reconciling the architecture with the scripts that exist.

**What held exactly as designed:** The engine. The world rules. The thematic architecture. The audience framework. The chime grammar. Unit as diagnostic instrument. The head tilt. The mirror architecture between characters.

**What the scripts built past the scaffolding:** E5 is not "Shear Wall" — Met trying to close the Noi investigation with math. E5 is "555" — Met leaving Bangkok, the field losing its dampening, Chloe discovering Met IS the variable while sitting in his bungalow holding a Hello Kitty keytag and talking to a dog. The fold happened in E5, not E6. The second bathroom is known. The reentry is what E6 holds, not the revelation.

**What this means for E6-E8:** The revelation happened early. The show doesn't explain it. The show walks back into the room where it happened and lives there.

## THE ENGINE (UNCHANGED)

Each episode follows the same structural pattern:

**A new case arrives.** A building that shouldn't be standing. An insurance claim that doesn't add up. A structural anomaly that defies engineering. Met investigates. Met SOLVES it — genuinely, brilliantly. The case closes. Everyone is satisfied.

**Except.** Chloe looks at the same data and sees what Met subtracted. His solution is technically correct but rationally stretched over something irrational. He solved the math. She's looking at the physics. Both right. Only one looking at the actual problem.

**The complete-but-incomplete pattern:** The audience gets a satisfying investigation AND an unsatisfying resolution. Met closes the case. Chloe opens it back up. Same data, different conclusion. Met isn't wrong — Met is incomplete. His reduction leaves a remainder. Chloe IS the remainder.

**E6-E8 ENGINE NOTE:** The procedural continues. Cases arrive. Met works them. The engine does not stop because Met's regulator cracked in Pai. The engine is how Met functions. The engine is Met's morning route in case form — the ritual that keeps the structure standing.

What changes: the transparency of the mask. Half the audience now sees through the procedural to the unraveling underneath. The other half sees the unraveling as temporary turbulence around a procedural that's holding. **Both are having the correct experience.** The show does not choose between them. The show runs both tracks simultaneously because that is what Met is doing — functioning AND dissolving, solving cases AND losing coherence, performing the reduction AND failing to reduce.

The procedural spine must remain thick enough for P1 through E7. Three investigative passes minimum. The cases in E6-E7 should be structurally resonant with Met's condition without being metaphorically obvious. The buildings are always real buildings with real problems. The buildings also happen to rhyme.

## THE RULES OF THE WORLD (UNCHANGED)

**The Coherence Field.** Bangkok has a pulse. At 8:12 every morning, for exactly one second, the city goes silent. Every city sound — traffic, construction, vendors, dogs, music — stops. Then resumes. No one notices except Met. He doesn't know why he notices. He doesn't talk about it. It's the first thing the audience sees (cold open, Episode 1) and the last thing they understand (finale).

**Static.** The wrongness in the world has a presence. Not a character. Not a monster. A CONDITION. Static manifests in the background: a building sign that reads "SERVICES" where no sign should be, a cooking show where pasta is made from pasta, a 7-11 chime that plays wrong. Static is always present. The camera doesn't linger on it. The audience catches it or they don't.

**The Buildings.** Met's cases are structural investigations, but the buildings are also metaphors. Each building that shouldn't stand represents something being held up by forces that shouldn't exist. As the series progresses, the connection between Met's unresolved grief and the structural impossibilities becomes clearer — not through exposition, but through pattern.

**Unit as Diagnostic Instrument.** If Unit reacts to something, it's real. If Unit ignores it, it's Met's imagination. The audience learns this rule without being told.

**The Head Tilt.** Same camera angle, same lens, same beat of hesitation before the head moves. Four times through E5 (Unit at TV, Met at 7-11, Met in dream, Unit at window sensing Met's return). Non-negotiable consistency. Same setup every time.

## 8-EPISODE ARC (REBUILT)

### Episode 1: "Load-Bearing"
The pilot. Cold open at 8:12. Met's world established through the morning route — Somchai's stall, monk's corner, the 7-11 (first wrong chime: HELLO WELCOMEHELLO), the rooster. First case: Khlong Toei building. Chloe arrives — the itch-finder, the remainder. Investigation engine demonstrated. Dream sequence: the shaving mirror, the smile, "Find me Daddy," the grey hallway. Unit watches the corner. Ice's filing system introduced — two cabinets, no acknowledgment.

**STATUS:** Script written. Exec note: case needs procedural structural review — currently solves too fast. Three investigative passes minimum required for P1 bonding. This is oxygen, not cosmetic.

### Episode 2: "Sathorn"
Second case deepens the engine. Met solves, Chloe finds the remainder. The audience sees the pattern and begins trusting it. Ice's filing system gets its first real test. Steve introduced — the grin, the stool, the warmth that costs something. The 8:12 moment happens again — this time the audience is WATCHING for it. Unit chase sequence. Bar scene at Margarita Storm establishes Steve's seat, the saved stool, the bottle as language.

**STATUS:** Script written. Exec note: needs strengthening to lock the engine before E3 begins complicating it.

### Episode 3: "Survey Lines"
Chloe's data mapping reveals geographic convergence across Met's old cases. The coin test — only works when Met is in the room. Kitty enters (phone call in E1 was the seed). Met and Chloe's working relationship shifts — she stops asking permission to look at closed files. The Banglamphu building hums at a frequency with no source. The Static's pattern begins emerging in Chloe's instruments.

**STATUS:** Script written.

### Episode 4: "Foundation"
Mid-season turn. A case hits close to the wound. Met's professional detachment cracks — the tourist freezing, the radius expanding past the city's membrane. Unit's behavior shifts: the search algorithm runs to completion in the soi (living child's voice through a wall), then Unit stops searching the bungalow. Goes to the corner. Lies down. Not exhaustion — completion. Ice makes a decision about the filing cabinets. The chime goes silent. Met walks away from the silence the way you walk away from a person who finally stops calling.

**STATUS:** Script written.

### Episode 5: "555"
*Ha ha ha.* Met leaves Bangkok. The field loses its dampening. Steve books tickets to Pai. The cold open: the camera walks Met's route alone, holding each station beat at the exact duration of the exchange that doesn't happen. WELCOME — one word where two used to be.

**Pai thread:** Train north. Steve's card game ("the game is whatever's happening between the people playing it"). The mountain road. Pai's bamboo and air — nothing for the hands to read. The dissolution: tokay (the dad laugh — first and only), Noi-cam (the walk from three feet, the before-face whole), the black corridor (Sound alone), the water ("Silly daddy!"), the fold (the second bathroom, water turning red, "no... no... no..."), the hard cut. Morning: the hand on bamboo finds nothing, the hand on his own chest finds heartbeat. "Different." The return train: Bangkok assembling at the window, Steve watching Met sleep, counting what's left.

**Bungalow thread (intercut):** Chloe at Met's bungalow with Unit. Unit settled — not searching, present. Chloe surrounded by evidence she doesn't know is evidence (shelf, hook, plate, unfaded square). THE CALL: Kitty on FaceTime, sitting up, showing off the room full of love that is going to kill her. The keytag waggle. White knuckles below frame. "Goodbye Katherine" / "It's Kitty, loser" / "Love you." Then to Unit: "That was my... me." The first reduction. The machine's first sound. Then: Chloe's walk on Met's route — the working chime she doesn't catch. Then: the office at night — the variable was never a variable, it was a constant. Met. Grief made electromagnetic. Shower-glass shape. Hands shaking. Unit at her feet, settled.

**Exit state:** Unit's head lifts. Same camera, same lens, same beat. Fourth head tilt. The city coming back. Met on a train, less protected. Bangkok waiting.

**STATUS:** Script complete. v5. Final.

### Episode 6: "Dead Load"
**THE REENTRY, NOT THE REVELATION.**

The fold happened. The audience knows. E6 does not explain. E6 does not acknowledge. E6 walks back into the bungalow and lets the room hold the weight of everything that isn't there.

Met walks back into the bungalow. The shelf. The hook. The single plate. The audience sees these for the sixth time and for the first time simultaneously. The show does not zoom in. The show does not pause. The show trusts the audience to carry what it gave them in E5.

**Met is not trying to recapture the old Met.** The trip to Pai did not heal him. The trip pulled the pin. What's emerging is forward-Met: a man whose regulator cracked and who is learning, for the first time, to function without the full reduction. Not recovered. Not broken-open in the prestige-drama way where damage becomes growth. Functioning differently.

### Episode 7: "Resonance"
**CONVERGENCE WITHOUT EXPOSITION.**

Everything the show has been building arrives in the same room. Not a reveal scene. Not a confrontation. The data, the chime, the buildings, the filing cabinets, the field, the 8:12 silence — all of it present, none of it explained.

**Steve:** The terror surfaces. Not a speech. Not a confession. A moment where the grin fails — not drops, fails. The grin tries to fire and can't and what's behind it is visible for two seconds before he recovers.

**Ice and the cabinets:** Not a scene where she says "I know what you are." A scene where the thin cabinet and the thick cabinet are both open and the audience can see what twenty years of protecting an impossible man looks like.

### Episode 8: "8:12"
**RECOGNITION, NOT REVELATION.**

The answer lands as "Oh." Not "So that's the rule."

The 8:12 silence is the show's thesis made temporal. What the city has been doing every morning — stopping, holding, resuming — is what grief does. What love does. What the space between a man and a city does when the man is one of the legs holding the table up.

**The chime resets.** HELLO WELCOME. Bright. Chirpy. Perfect. The exact sound from E1. The audience's skin crawls. The casual audience hears a normal chime and feels nothing. The complicit audience understands: the Static's endgame is not silence, not chaos. A perfect copy. Indistinguishable from the original unless you were paying attention from the beginning.

**Resolution:** Emotionally complete. Mythologically open. The door stays cracked for the audience. Met is not fixed. Met is whole. Whole includes broken.

**Post-credits:** Seeds expansion without requiring it. The show is complete. The world isn't.

## THEMATIC ARCHITECTURE (CONFIRMED BY E1-E5)

**The show is about reduction.** Met is a genius because he reduces complex systems to solvable equations. This is also his fatal flaw. He reduced his grief to a case file. He reduced his daughter to an unsolved problem. He reduced Bangkok to a dataset. The show asks: what happens when you stop subtracting the things that don't fit?

**Grief as structural failure.** A building that shouldn't stand is a person who shouldn't function. Met functions. He works. He solves. He eats. He walks his dog. He is structurally sound. And something fundamental is wrong with the foundation that no one can see — except a dog and a woman with a spreadsheet.

**Found family through parallel damage.** Met, Chloe, Ice, and Unit don't bond. They recognize each other. Each is carrying something they've reduced to manageable size. The show is about what happens when the reductions stop holding.

**Forward, not backward.** Met's arc is not recovery. Met does not recapture the before-Met. What emerges is forward-Met: a man whose regulator cracked and who learns to function without the full reduction. The machine doesn't get rebuilt. The machine becomes transparent.
