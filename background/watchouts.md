## VALIDATED HARD GAPS
*These survived four rounds of recalibration and a 20% happy-pathing audit. They're real.*

### 1. E1 CASE — THREE PASSES [HIGHEST PRIORITY]
Khlong Toei case currently solves in one pass. Met walks in, touches wall, delivers monologue, done. The procedural door needs weight. This is the first ten pages any exec reads.

**Needed:**
- Pass 1: Fast first read that looks like a solve
- Pass 2: Contradiction that reopens (Chloe's presence should complicate)
- Pass 3: Final solve that satisfies but leaves the gap only the camera sees

This gap unlocks everything. Without it, the audience doesn't believe the engine, and the show can't subvert an engine they haven't internalized.

### 2. ICE — UNMEDIATED MOMENT [HIGH]
Every Ice scene in E1-E3 is observed through glass, walls, other cameras. The design is correct but needs one break. Thirty seconds where the camera is in the room with her. Not the sigh through the wall (camera's moment). Something that belongs to the actress.

**Why:** For Yaya. An actress getting three episodes of sighs and cabinet locks before her scene needs something to build on. Also: the audience needs to hold Ice as a person, not infrastructure, before the degaussing asks them to hold her through destruction.

**Placement:** E2 or E3. Before E4.

### 3. E5 RECOMPOSITION — STARVED [HIGH]
Dissolution gets four fully realized layers (tokay, walk, black, water/fold). Recomposition gets a page and a half. Two more pages needed.

**Specific gaps:**
- Porch scene needs one paragraph of Pai-at-2-AM sensory detail before dialogue
- "Different" needs a physical action after the word — something small that earns time
- Return train may need minor extension after Steve's face-assessment

Not more story. More air. The audience needs to set the fold down.

### 4. E5 CHLOE DISCOVERY — NEEDS BREATH [HIGH]
Fold's emotional debris is still falling when 12B arrives. Need a beat between the hard cut and Chloe's office. Even ten seconds of Bangkok ambient. The city existing. Then Chloe. Then the shape rings instead of getting swallowed.

### 5. E2 — WEAKEST EPISODE [HIGH]
The episode that holds the audience after the pilot. Currently thin or incomplete relative to E1, E3, E4. The Kitty-Chloe video call is the best naturalistic dialogue in the series — build around it. Case needs to be as present as E1's and E4's.

### 6. MET CONTAINMENT BEATS — E2 AND E3 [HIGH]
Three more physical moments where the body betrays the machine. Active stillness, not passivity. The coat hook is one. The shelf reach is one. Need three more across E2-E3.

Small. Involuntary. The body ahead of the brain. The half-step at Somchai's stall is the model. Without these, five episodes of stillness reads as inertia on screen regardless of design philosophy.

### 7. DOCUMENT ROUTING — PIPE SEPARATION [MEDIUM]
Current prosey scripts carry four streams in one pipe:
- DP/sound script (stays dense — their dialogue)
- Audience-experience scaffolding ("First watch / On rewatch" blocks — moves to private showrunner bible)
- The Constitution (face rule, sound rule, threshold — standalone document for department heads)
- Actor sides (25 words — separate document)

Not cutting. Routing. Each piece goes to the cast that needs it.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Bangkok Requirement
The show REQUIRES BKK. Cannot be "a Southeast Asian city." Specificity IS the point. The 7-11 chime is a REAL SOUND. Temple chanting specific to Theravada Buddhism. Soi layout, Sukhumvit chaos are the diagnostic toolkit, not set dressing.

### Thai Government Support
Thai tourism budget aligns. Real Thailand, real jobs, real locations. Existing TTT relationships. The bible needs to frame BKK as a production ADVANTAGE, not a risk.

### Budget Scalability
The Static scales from cheap (desaturation, generic labels) to expensive (geometric dissolution). The show's horror is blandness, not spectacle.
