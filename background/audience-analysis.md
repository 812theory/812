## TARGET AUDIENCE PERSONAS

### PERSONA 1: THE PROCEDURAL LOYALIST
**Who:** Weekly TV viewer. Watched all of House, probably some Sherlock, maybe Monk. 35-55. Comfort-watches procedurals. Likes feeling smart when the diagnosis lands. Not opposed to depth but doesn't require it — the puzzle is the draw.

**What they're looking for:** Case-of-the-week satisfaction. Brilliant protagonist with a flaw. A world they can settle into on Tuesday night. Predictable structure with enough variation to not feel stale. They want to know the rules of the show by Episode 2 and feel rewarded for learning them.

**Their version of the pitch:** "House in Bangkok."

**ENGINE NOTE:** The case-of-the-week must be thick enough to sustain this viewer. Three investigative passes minimum: fast first read → contradiction that expands the mystery → final solve that satisfies but leaves a gap only the camera sees. If Met solves the case in 5 minutes, you've got a cold open, not an engine. E1 needs a procedural structural review to meet this standard.

### PERSONA 2: THE PRESTIGE REWATCHER
**Who:** The person who paused Severance to screenshot the hallway numbers. Watched Sixth Sense twice in a weekend. Builds theories on Reddit. 25-45. Quality over quantity. Will wait a year for a show worth their time. Cares about craft — notices sound design, catches color grading shifts, sees the locked camera and feels it in their stomach before they can name why.

**What they're looking for:** Architecture. A show that trusts them. Details that detonate on rewatch. The satisfaction of catching something nobody else caught. They want to feel smarter on second watch, not just more informed. They want the show to be ABOUT something, not just depicting something.

**Their version of the pitch:** "Built like The Sixth Sense, sustained across a full season."

### PERSONA 3: THE EMOTIONAL ARCHAEOLOGIST
**Who:** The person who cried at the Pixar opening of Up and has never fully recovered. Watched Fleabag's fourth wall break and felt personally addressed. 25-50. Skews female but not exclusively. Reads for character, not plot. Will forgive structural looseness if the emotional truth is precise. Gets annoyed when shows use trauma as decoration rather than architecture.

**What they're looking for:** A character they can't stop thinking about. Emotional specificity — not "he's sad," but the specific shape of his sadness. The Hello Kitty keychain moment. The untouched second drink. Details that carry more weight than they appear to. They want to feel something they haven't felt watching TV before, and they want the show to earn it.

**Their version of the pitch:** "In the emotional register of Severance."

## FUNNEL DYNAMICS

P1 (broadest) gets a 9/10 entry in E1 and naturally filters. Most stay, some convert to P2, some drop at E5. You don't build a prestige show for the procedural loyalist. You build them a door.

P2 is the engine. Rewatch audience, Reddit theorists, Sunday posters. Show is architecturally designed for them. They're your marketing department.

P3 is the awards audience. Kitty keychain, second drink, sound design. What makes critics write essays.

**Gap to watch:** P1 drops from 8.0 to 6.8 at the breaking moment. E6-E7 must retain SOME procedural structure or P1 exits.

## THAI AUDIENCE

Cross-cuts all three personas. Brings context international audience doesn't have.

**Unit-cam:** Thai viewer who feels they've lost something to westernization will FEEL the full Bangkok without naming it. Interpreted as national pride or "finally someone sees Thailand, not like The Hangover." Reduced in expression, deep on the inside — that's Thai culture.

**Steve's too-precise moment:** Thai audiences catch historical reference as school knowledge. Two men nodding through it like shared memory registers as uncanny before international audience even notices.

**The show's argument:** Bangkok's chaos is its immune system. The specificity is what's worth saving. A love letter Thai audiences should recognize as real.

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

1. Cases must be thick enough to sustain P1 through E1-E5. Three investigative passes minimum.
2. Every episode plants 3-5 details that only detonate on rewatch (P2 fuel).
3. Every episode contains at least one moment of emotional specificity that carries more weight than it appears (P3 fuel).
4. E2 is where the show's gravitational field turns on (Unit chase + Steve bar scene). E1 plants the mystery. E2 gives the mystery MASS.
5. The funnel narrows by design. Losing some P1 at E5 is correct.
