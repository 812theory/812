## PRODUCTION NOTES — RETAINED IN FULL

### The Fold

No depiction of Noi's death. No depiction of her mother's death. Red water, a man on the floor, three syllables. That is all it will ever contain. If someone in a room suggests going further — stop. You are in the room where the show is being ended.

### Sound — The Bathroom

One microphone. One room. No processing. No score. No reverb. No distortion added. The perceived wrongness is the removal of everything Sound built across five episodes. DO NOT FIX IT. Show this note to whoever flags it. If they flag it again: find someone else.

### Her Face

Never shown. Not in E6. Not in E8. Not in the finale. Not in any version of this show. Hair, back, hands, height only. The show is Met. The show cannot look. Page-one rule in every script: "Pai is visible. Her face is not."

### Noi-cam

Her eyes, her height, her world. Used in the tokay, the walk, and inside the tub. Never used again. The show's most sacred perspective.

### The Dad Laugh

First and only time the audience hears Met laugh. The before-Met in one sound. The actor has never produced this sound in any previous scene. The audience will not be prepared for it.

### Noi's Lines

Two. Tokay scene: her gecko guess and discovery giggle. Water scene: "Silly daddy!" Both unreconstructable. Both proof she was real.

### The Hard Cut

The show's first and only hard cut. Out of the second bathroom. The camera's survival reflex. The show is not sorry.

### Unit's Head-Tilt

The fourth in the series. Same camera. Same lens. Same beat. Hold it. Do not add music. Do not cut early. The audience's body will do the rest.

### Steve's Cosmic Weight

Steve is not simply a friend. The actor needs to know Steve has been doing this — holding space, saving stools, booking escape routes — for longer than a human lifetime. Steve's stillness is not peace. Steve's stillness is a man holding very still because movement would be panic. He has been doing this for longer than Bangkok. The before includes other men on other porches and other trips that went wrong.

### E5 Chime Grammar

Cold open — Met's 7-11: WELCOME (one word, HELLO dropped). Hua Lamphong 7-11: HELLO WELCOME, clean and correct (edge of the radius). Met's route during Chloe's walk: clean and correct (Met's gone, field absent). The field has a radius.

### The Call — Key Notes

No words for the greeting — keytag and cat sticker only. First spoken word: "You're UP." "Chlo" (no E) is Kitty's word only. Monitor beep present under every line — neither twin acknowledges it. "Quiet's not bad" has two registers — trust the room. "Goodbye Katherine" / "It's Kitty, loser" / "Love you" — the names do the work. "My... me" is the machine's first sound. The show never puts both animals in the same frame.

### Handoff to E6

The camera that broke in the fold has to decide in E6 who it is now. The DP needs to know this before the first shot of E6 is blocked. When Met walks back into the bungalow in E6: do not add music, do not let the camera be kind, do not soften the reentry. That scene is the show's reason for existing.
