# 8:12 THEORY
## Episode 2: "Sathorn"

EXT. SUKHUMVIT ROAD — BANGKOK — MORNING

Same soi. Same man. Same walk. The audience knows it now.

Somchai's cart on the corner.

SOMCHAI
(Thai, subtitled, grinning)
Hey, handsome man!

MET
Still not funny.

SOMCHAI
(calling after him)
Still funny!

CAMERA holds on Somchai a beat after Met passes — the wok, the photo, the stool.

[ grief-seed framing — removed ]

TITLE CARD: 8:12 AM

One second. City goes silent. Met's stride hitches — recognition, not alarm.

CAMERA: not on Met. A jasmine garland vendor three meters away is frozen mid-motion, eyes open, looking directly at Met. Nobody else stopped. She doesn't know she stopped. Met doesn't know she was looking.

Bangkok resumes.

---

EXT. SOI — CONTINUOUS

Unit is with Met. Walking. Then: a LOCK — every muscle rigid. Head turns toward something with no sensory category a human would recognize.

He runs. Full sprint. Gone.

MET
Unit! UNIT!

Met follows, winded. By the time he catches up, Unit is standing at a corner. Perfectly still. Staring at empty space. Nothing there.

MET
(bent over, hands on knees)
What the HELL was that?

Unit looks at the corner. Looks at Met. Looks at the corner. Turns and walks back the way he came. Calm. As if nothing happened.

Met stares at the corner. Just a corner. He follows the dog home.

---

INT. MET'S OFFICE — MORNING

Ice already there. Coffee at the edge of the desk. Met grabs it. Chloe at her desk — her desk now, a new hook installed for her coat, away from the one near the unfaded square. Ice handled this. Neither woman discussed it.

MET
Dog lost his mind this morning.

ICE
(not looking up — a fact, not a comfort)
He. Does. That.

[ Ice's subtext / what the periods mean — removed ]

Phone rings. Ice answers. Her posture changes — the spine-straighten of someone hearing a name they've been expecting.

ICE
(to Met, hanging up)
Sathorn Unique. Insurance company. Structural anomaly in the survey.

MET
Sathorn's been abandoned for thirty years.

ICE
That's the anomaly.

She hands him the file. Met opens it. His eyes do the thing. Chloe is already at her tablet — both of them working the problem in parallel, silent, without acknowledging each other.

CAMERA: stationary against the wall, catching both in the same frame. The audience feels the collaboration before anyone acknowledges it.

Ice opens the thick filing cabinet. Pulls documents. More than seems necessary for a building inspection.

NOTE: the thick cabinet protrudes past the thin one by a couple of inches. Visual fact. File it.

---

EXT. SATHORN UNIQUE TOWER — STREET LEVEL — DAY

Forty-nine stories of exposed concrete. Rebar reaching into the sky like bones through skin. Open sky where walls should be. Construction halted 1997. The Ghost Tower. Bangkok knows it. Nobody thinks about it.

CAMERA stays across the street — two small figures entering something enormous and unfinished.

Inside: dark, humid. Graffiti. A mattress. Bottles. The archaeology of people who live in the gaps.

Met stands in the center. Looks up through forty-nine floors of skeleton — Bangkok's sky visible at the top like looking through the ribcage of something that died standing. He starts working.

He moves fast. His eyes find cracks, columns, density changes. He taps walls and listens. A load-bearing column: hairline fractures in a pattern that should indicate failure but don't. Something arrested the decay.

MET
(to Chloe, already moving)
Reinforcement work. Post-construction. Somebody's trying to bring it back without anyone noticing. The density pattern is consistent with injected concrete — standard rehabilitation technique, lousy execution. Call the insurance company and tell them their anomaly is a renovation they don't know about.

Chloe at her tablet. The itch-finder firing.

CHLOE
Your load analysis is perfect. Except the density readings are inside the original concrete. Not added to it. Through it. Like the building grew denser from within.

MET
Measurement error. Calibration drift on the insurance company's equipment.

Already on the stairs. Case mostly solved. Moving on. Chloe looks at the wall. Looks at her tablet. Looks at the wall. Doesn't argue. Files it.

---

INT. MET'S OFFICE — AFTERNOON

Ice alone. A knock. DSI AGENT — badge visible, smile easy.

DSI AGENT
(Thai, subtitled)
Routine verification. The Sathorn Unique survey — we need to confirm credentials for the structural consultant on record.

ICE
(already reaching for a folder)
Of course.

She produces the mountain. A professional biography that accounts for exactly as many years as a man who looks fifty should have. The agent reviews the three pages that matter. Nods.

DSI AGENT
(satisfied, standing)
Everything looks fine. Thank you for your time.

He leaves. Ice closes the door.

One beat. Something in that interaction cost her. Not fear — fatigue.

She goes to the thick filing cabinet. Opens it.

NOTE: the thick cabinet no longer protrudes past the thin one. They're flush. Side by side. Even.

Ice finds her file. Closes the cabinet. Walks away.

CAMERA holds on the two cabinets for half a beat after she's gone.

---

EXT. SATHORN UNIQUE — STREET LEVEL — LATE AFTERNOON

Chloe came back alone. Standing across from the Ghost Tower. Her investigation — not checking Met's work. The twenty percent he dismissed is her hundred percent.

A vendor selling trinkets nearby. Chloe's eye catches a HELLO KITTY KEYCHAIN. She buys it without thinking. Shoves it in her pocket.

[ audience split — one person files this, everyone else sees a keychain — removed ]

---

INT. SATHORN UNIQUE — UPPER FLOORS — LATE AFTERNOON

Chloe works the building alone. Maps the density anomaly. Confirms it — the readings ARE inside the original concrete. Not added. Through. She follows wall by wall, floor by floor.

A pattern emerges. Geometric. Beautiful. Makes no sense.

She maps it. Rotates the model. Zooms in, zooms out. The geometry holds at every scale. She exports the data. Puts the tablet away. Stands in forty-nine floors of abandoned concrete with Bangkok visible through the missing walls.

---

INT. MET'S OFFICE — EARLY EVENING

Chloe puts her tablet on Met's desk. The geometric model on screen.

CHLOE
Your renovation theory is clean. The density pattern supports it. But the geometry doesn't.

Met looks at the model. Rotates. His jaw works. Processing.

MET
Moisture migration.

CHLOE
Excuse me?

MET
Bangkok's humidity is specific. Ninety percent average. Combined with the temperature differential between exposed concrete and interior mass, you get a moisture gradient that drives mineral transport through the capillary structure of the original pour. Over thirty years of seasonal cycling, the minerals consolidate along stress lines — which happen to follow geometric patterns because that's how crystalline structures form under directional pressure.

CHLOE
That's beautiful.

MET
It's chemistry.

CHLOE
And it explains about eighty percent of what I'm seeing.

MET
Eighty percent is a passing grade.

CHLOE
Not in structural engineering it isn't.

Beat. Met looks at her. Not annoyed — something else. He looks at the model again.

MET
We go back tomorrow.

---

EXT. SATHORN UNIQUE — UPPER FLOORS — NEXT DAY

They climb together. Bangkok visible through missing walls at every level — the skyline shifting as they rise, sound fading with altitude. By the twentieth floor: only wind through exposed rebar and their footsteps on raw concrete.

Met works. Deep now — past the surface theory, into the structure itself. His complete answer forms.

MET
(dictating into phone)
Densification attributable to pozzolanic reaction between original aggregate chemistry and sustained moisture cycling. Rate consistent with thirty-year environmental exposure. Structural capacity exceeds original design spec by approximately fourteen percent. Recommend insurance company revise survey methodology. Building is not deteriorating.
(beat)
Building is maturing.

Done. Case solved.

Thirty-second floor. Met walks past an interior wall.

SOUND: the building's ambient acoustic environment changes when Met passes this wall — warmer, a low resonance not present ten feet ago. Met doesn't notice.

CAMERA: Chloe's tablet flickers — one data point spikes for a single measurement cycle and returns to normal. She's not looking at the screen. She's watching Met.

The data point disappears into the scroll. They descend.

Ground floor. Bangkok hits them — heat, noise, light.

Met stops at the entrance. Turns. Looks back up at the building. Half a second too long. Not concern. Something he can't name — the feeling of having answered every question and not being finished.

He shakes it off. Walks to the car. Chloe watches him look up. Files it.

---

INT. MET'S BUNGALOW — EVENING

Met on the couch. TV on — a documentary about the Bangkok BTS Skytrain smart card. A narrator discusses the rounded corners of the 2014 Rabbit card versus the 2011 version. Nobody would watch this. Met is not watching this.

Unit at his feet.

Through the window, the 7-11 at the end of the soi. Doors open for a customer.

**"HELLO WEWELCOME."**

Wrong. Different wrong than Episode 1 — the stutter has migrated. HELLO still leads but WE and WELCOME have collapsed into each other. Same level of wrongness. Different shape.

Met doesn't react.

Unit watches the corner near the window. Through it — barely visible through the evening haze — the top of Sathorn Unique on the skyline.

---

EXT. MARGARITA STORM — NIGHT

Open air bar. Bangkok at night. Tokay geckos screaming somewhere overhead — invisible, eternal.

The 7-11 across the street: doors cycle, chime audible.

**"HELLO WELCOME."**

Correct. Clean. The wrongness is somewhere else.

Margarita Storm. Accordion wooden panels open to the street. Bangkok is in the bar.

Met at the bar. Not working. Not solving. Having a drink.

STEVE already there. Two Singha bottles, two glasses. And between them — slightly to Steve's side, in front of an empty stool — a SECOND DRINK. Poured once. Untouched. Condensation running. The bartender doesn't clear it. Steve doesn't look at it.

CAMERA anchors on the second drink throughout the scene. Steve moves; the camera keeps the drink center-frame.

STEVE
(easy grin)
Hey — you're the building guy, right?

MET
Depends on the building.

STEVE
(laughs)
Fair enough. Steve.

MET
Met.

The expat bar ritual. How long, where from, why Thailand. A round for the house — rice whiskey shots appear. Both reach for one automatically. Cheers. Down. The synchronized wince.

The conversation flows. Khao soi. Tailors. Singha versus Chang. The tokay screams.

STEVE
I will NEVER get used to that.

MET
(dry almost-smile)
You're not supposed to.

Met at the bar — precision gone, reduction gone. A man having a drink. The version that existed before grief built the machine on top of him.

[ "Met has a before" / House/Sherlock comparison — removed ]

Steve tells a story — taxi driver, wrong airport, Don Mueang instead of Suvarnabhumi, 4 AM, pidgin Thai. Met is laughing. Actually laughing.

STEVE
(mid-story, waving his Singha)
Well. Not the real capital.

A scoff-laugh. Already past it. Met nods — reflex, not reaction. The conversation keeps moving.

CAMERA holds on the glass — both glasses, the one Met is drinking and the one nobody is drinking. One beat. Two. Longer than any shot in this scene has held. Then widens.

[ "the camera heard something / neither man reacted / the camera did" — removed ]

The conversation fades. Camera pulls back through the open wooden wall, out to the street. The bar: a frame of warm light. Two silhouettes. Bangkok in the foreground.

And there — on the sidewalk, among the foot traffic — UNIT. Small. Golden. Patient. Watching the street. Not anxious. Present.

Met emerges.

MET
Good meeting you.

STEVE
Yeah. See you around, building guy.

Met walks into the night. Unit falls in beside him — unhurried, the rhythm of two creatures who share a direction without discussing it. They disappear around a corner.

Camera stays with Steve. He watches them go until they turn the corner and are gone.

He looks at the empty stool. Looks at the untouched drink. The grin is still there — but something underneath it shifts. The grin costs something now.

He picks up his Singha. Takes a long drink. Puts it down.

Doesn't touch the other glass.

---

INT. CHLOE'S APARTMENT — NIGHT

Small. Rented. Serious about being here. Hasn't committed to furniture.

Chloe on the bed. Video call. On screen: KITTY — the same face rearranged by a different life. Warmer. Brighter. In a hospital bed. Her energy brighter than it should be.

KITTY
(Australian, warmer than Chloe's)
So you're telling me you FLEW to Bangkok because some bloke's MATHS were wrong?

CHLOE
His maths are fine. His conclusions are wrong.

KITTY
That's the same thing.

CHLOE
It is absolutely not the same thing.

They laugh. The ease of thirty years.

CAMERA: under the desk, below frame — Chloe's hand, gripping the Hello Kitty keychain. Knuckles white.

KITTY
Things going okay though? Bangkok treating you alright?

CHLOE
Yeah. Yeah, things are... clicking. The work is good. The city is insane. My boss is —

KITTY
Fit?

CHLOE
He's fifty.

KITTY
And?

CHLOE
And he's my boss. And he's impossible. And he solves things that shouldn't be solvable and then acts like it's nothing.

Kitty grins — she heard everything Chloe didn't say.

KITTY
So he's fit.

CHLOE
Kitty, loser. Love you.

KITTY
(laughing, coughing slightly, recovering)
Love you.

The call ends. Screen goes dark. Chloe's reflection stares back. The keychain in her hand. The apartment quiet. Bangkok hums outside.

---

EXT. SUKHUMVIT ROAD — MORNING

Dawn. Met walking. Same route, shorthand — Somchai's wave, the monk, the rooster, the traffic light.

TITLE CARD: 8:12 AM

One second. Silence.

CAMERA: not on Met. Not on the city. On Unit — at home, in the bungalow hallway. During the silence, his ears go up. Not startled. Listening. To something in the gap.

Bangkok resumes. Unit's ears go down. He goes back to watching the corner.
