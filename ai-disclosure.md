# AI Disclosure

8:12 Theory was written with AI in the room.

Three tools were used across the development of this show: Claude (Anthropic),
Versey.ai, and SelfActual.ai. None of them wrote it. All of them were present
while it was being written.

They were thinking partners. They pushed back on bad ideas. They held
context when the sessions got long. They asked questions that opened things
up. In a small number of cases — estimated at 1.5 to 2.5 percent of the
total text — a suggested phrase or sentence was kept because it was right.

The architecture is mine. The grief is mine. The specificity of Bangkok at
2 AM is mine. The dog searching empty rooms every night for someone who isn't
coming back — that came from a real place that no tool can reach.

I'm not running an AI-cleanup pass on the scripts. The work reads the way
it reads because of what it came from, not despite it. The point was never
to produce clean copy. The point was to make something true.

If you're a writer reading this: the AI didn't make it easier to write. It
made it possible to keep going. That's a different thing, and it's worth
being honest about.

— Jay Wright
